# Ai_Layoffs_Dashbord
AI_Dashboard
Global Employment Trends Dashboard (2023–2024): Automation & AI Impact Analysis
📊 Overview & Key Metrics
This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of global employment disruptions, specifically focusing on layoffs, automation risks, and regional/industry-specific impacts between 2023 and 2024.

High-Level KPIs
Total Employee Pool Evaluated: 151,700

Total Layoffs: 24,430

Overall Automation Risk Assessment: High * Estimated AI Impact Index: 162.57

Average Layoff %: 303.33% (Note: This metric likely represents an aggregated or calculated index score across multiple sub-sectors rather than a literal percentage).

Automation Risk Score: 1,000

🔍 Key Insights & Visual Breakdowns
1. Geographic Impact (Layoffs by Country)
The impact of automation and economic shifting varies significantly by region, with Western tech hubs seeing the highest volume of workforce reductions.

United States (USA): Leading global layoffs by a massive margin with 9,180 recorded job cuts.

India: The second hardest-hit region with 5,150 layoffs, heavily driven by its large tech and operations sectors.

Europe & Asia-Pacific: Germany (3,600), Japan (2,800), and the UK (2,100) follow, while Canada shows the lowest impact among evaluated countries at 1,600.

2. Temporal Trends (Year-over-Year Comparison)
Layoffs are accelerating as AI and automation tools become more deeply integrated into enterprise workflows.

2023: 10,250 layoffs

2024: 14,180 layoffs

Insight: Layoffs grew by ~38.3% year-over-year, signaling that the disruption is compounding rather than slowing down.

3. Layoffs by Risk Level
The data categorizes job roles by their inherent vulnerability to being automated.

High Risk: 16,730 layoffs (The vast majority of workforce reductions are happening in roles highly susceptible to automation).

Medium Risk: 6,150 layoffs.

Low Risk: 1,550 layoffs (Demonstrating that highly specialized or human-centric roles remain relatively insulated).

4. Role-Specific Vulnerabilities
The horizontal bar chart identifies specific job titles bearing the brunt of the cuts:

Most Affected Roles: Customer Support and Technicians experience the highest volume of layoffs, as generative AI tools, chatbots, and automated diagnostics replace routine tasks.

Tech & Engineering Cuts: Software Engineers, Cloud Engineers, and Assembly Workers also show significant layoff volumes, reflecting industry-wide corrections and automated code/infrastructure deployment tools.

Least Affected Roles: Admin Staff, Data Scientists, and Security Analysts see lower layout numbers, indicating sustained or growing corporate reliance on data interpretation and security.
